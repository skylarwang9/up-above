2020 May 30: SpaceX makes history as the first private company to launch astronauts into space.






2019 April 10:The first image of a black hole was captured by the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT).

2015 July: NASA's New Horizons arrives at Pluto.






2012 October 14: Australian skydiver Felix Baumgartner becomes the first human being to break the sound barrier without a jet or spacecraft as he jumps from the edge of space.






2012 August 6: NASA's Curiosity rover successfully landed on Mars.






2011 November 26: NASA launched Curiosity, the biggest, best equipped robot ever sent to explore another planet.






2008 May 25: NASA’s Phoenix Mars Lander landed safely and began sending images home. Scientists later reported that Phoenix discovered chunks of ice.






2007 October 4: The Space Age turns 50, five decades after the historic launch of Sputnik 1.






2007 August 8: NASA's Space Shuttle Endeavour launches toward the International Space Station on the STS-118 construction mission.






2005 October 12: A two-taikonaut crew launches aboard the Chinese Shenzhou 6.






2005 July 26: Space Shuttle Discovery was launched with seven astronauts aboard; this was America’s first manned space shot since the 2003 Columbia disaster.






2003 October 15: Yang Liwei becomes China's first taikonaut, having launched aboard Shenzhou 5.






2001 August 29: Japan's workhorse launch system, the two-stage H-2A rocket, launches for the first time.






1999 November 19: China successfully test launches the unmanned Shenzhou 1.






1995 February 3: Eileen Collins became the first female Shuttle pilot.






1990 April 25: The Space Shuttle Discovery releases the Hubble Space Telescopeinto Earth orbit.






1990 April 7: China launches the Asiasat-1 communications satellite, completing its first commercial contract.






1986 February 20: The Soviet Union launches the Mir space station, the first consistently inhabited space station, remained in orbit unti 2001.






1983 June 19 - Sally Ride became the first American woman in space on Challenger’s second mission.






1981 April 12: Space Shuttle Columbia lifts off from Cape Canaveral, beginning the first space mission for NASA's new astronaut transportation system.






1980 July 18: India launches its Rohini 1 satellite. By using its domestically developed SLV-3 rocket, India becomes the seventh nation capable of sending objects into space by itself.






1979 December 24: The French-built Ariane rocket, Europe's first launch vehicle, launches successfully.






1979 September 1: Pioneer 11 becomes the first spacecraft to fly past Saturn.






1977 August & September：Voyagers 1 and 2 were launched.






1976 July 20: The U.S. Viking 1 lands on Mars, becoming the first successful Mars lander.






1975 July 17： The American Apollo 18 and Soviet Soyuz 19 dock in the Apollo-Soyuz Test Project.






1973 May 14： The U.S. launched its first space station, Skylab.






1971 November 13： The Mariner 9 probe became the first craft to orbit another world - Mars.






1971 April 19: The Soviet space station Salyut 1 was launched.






1969 July 16: The Apollo 11 spacecraft landed on the Moon and Neil Armstrong became the first man to walk on the Moon.






1965 July 14: The spacecraft Mariner 4 transmitted the first pictures of Mars.






1965 March 18: While tethered to his spacecraft, Russian cosmonaut Alexi Leonov became the first man to walk in space.






1963 June 16: Russian cosmonaut Valentina Nikolayeva Tereshkova became the first woman in space.






1962 February 20: Astronaut John Glenn became the first American in orbit.






1961 May 25: President Kennedy challenged the country to put a man on the moon by the end of the decade.






1961 May 5: Astronaut Alan Shepard became the first American in space.






1961 April 12: Russian cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space and the first human to orbit the Earth.






1960 August 19: The Soviet craft Sputnik 5 was launched, carrying the dogs Strelka and Belka. They became the first living beings to survive a trip into space.






1959 August 7: NASA's Explorer 6 launches and provides the first photographs of the Earth from space.






1959 January 2: The U.S.S.R. launches Luna 1, which misses the moon but becomes the first artificial object to leave Earth orbit.






1958 January 31: Explorer 1, the first satellite with an onboard telemetry system, is launched by the United States into orbit aboard a Juno rocket and returns data from space.






1957 November 3: The Soviet spacecraft Sputnik 2 was launched with a dog named Laika on board. Laika did not survive the voyage.






1957 October 4: The Soviet Union launched the first satellite, Sputnik, into space.
